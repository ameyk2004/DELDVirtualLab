Binary Coded Decimal (BCD) Adder

A Binary Coded Decimal (BCD) Adder is a digital circuit designed to perform addition on Binary Coded Decimal numbers. BCD is a binary representation of decimal numbers, where each decimal digit is represented by a fixed number of bits, typically four bits.

BCD Addition Principle

BCD addition follows the principles of binary addition with additional considerations for decimal carries. In BCD representation, each digit is represented by a four-bit binary code. When adding two BCD numbers, the addition is performed bit-wise, and if the sum of two BCD digits exceeds 9 (1001 in binary), a decimal carry is generated.

BCD Adder Architecture

The BCD Adder typically consists of multiple full-adder circuits organized to handle the addition of each pair of corresponding bits in the BCD numbers. Additionally, it includes logic to manage carry propagation between adjacent BCD digits, ensuring proper decimal arithmetic.

Carry Propagation in BCD Addition

Carry propagation is a crucial aspect of BCD addition. When the sum of two BCD digits is greater than 9 (1001 in binary), a carry is generated and added to the next higher-order digit. This process ensures that the addition follows the rules of decimal arithmetic, preventing errors in the BCD representation.
