Full Adder and Half Adder

Theory

Binary Adder is a combinational logic circuit which is used to perform binary addition and subtraction .Full adder is a little more difficult to implement than a half-adder. The full-adder has three inputs and two outputs. The first two inputs are A and B and the third input is an input carry designated as CIN. When full adder logic is designed we will be able to string eight of them together to create a byte-wide adder and cascade the carry bit from one adder to the next
